# Welcome to Your Contractor Payment Kit — Colorado

Thank you for your purchase. This kit contains four documents: this instruction packet, a demand-for-payment letter, a Notice of Intent to File a Mechanic's Lien, and a Statement of Mechanic's Lien. It is built to help you pursue payment for work you have already done on a property in Colorado.

**This kit is not ready to file as-is.** Read that again. The three filing documents you are about to download include visible yellow "CUSTOMER VERIFICATION REQUIRED" callouts wherever Colorado law requires you to confirm specific language against the current statute. Before you file anything, you will:

- Read this instruction packet all the way through.
- Look up every statute section referenced in a yellow callout at **https://leg.colorado.gov/colorado-revised-statutes** and confirm the language in your document matches the current statute.
- Fill in any blanks (including the legal description of the property, if you did not have it during intake).
- Sign the lien statement in front of a notary public (see the Notary Guide section).
- Send the demand letter and Notice of Intent by certified mail with return receipt requested (see the Certified Mail How-To section).
- File the lien statement with the county recorder's office in the county where the property is located (not necessarily where you live).

**What this service is and is not.** Earth Pro Connect LLC is not a law firm. We do not review your case. We do not give you legal advice about whether to file, how much to demand, or what happens if the owner disputes the lien. Mechanic's lien law has many traps; the Iron Law is that improperly filed liens can expose you to liability under C.R.S. § 38-22-128 and to claims for slander of title. If your facts are complex, or if you are past any deadline, most contractors in this situation call a Colorado-licensed attorney before using a kit like this one.

**Time and cost to complete.** Most contractors using this kit spend 3–8 hours of their own time across 2–4 weeks (the calendar time is mostly waiting periods required by law — 10 business days for the demand, then 10 days for the Notice of Intent). Out-of-pocket costs beyond what you paid for this kit:

- Notary: $5–$25 per signing (UPS Store, bank, AAA office).
- Certified mail with return receipt: roughly $8–$12 per mailing. You will send at least two.
- County filing fee for the lien statement: varies by county — see the County Filing Directory section. Most Colorado counties fall in the $13–$30 range for a 1–2 page document, but confirm by calling the recorder before you go.

**What this kit does not cover.** Foreclosing on the lien to actually collect (that requires a lawsuit and an attorney), release-of-lien forms when you get paid, bonding around a lien, liens against public property, homestead properties without a pre-work contract, and any situation where the disputed amount is large or contested. If any of those apply, the "When to call an attorney" section of this packet lays out the rough cost ranges.

If you have questions about what is or is not included in this kit, call a Colorado-licensed attorney before proceeding.
