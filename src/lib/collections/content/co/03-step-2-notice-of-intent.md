# Step 2 — Notice of Intent to File a Mechanic's Lien

The Notice of Intent (NOI) is the second filing document in your kit. C.R.S. § 38-22-109(3) requires you to serve a 10-day notice before filing the lien statement. Skipping this step, or getting the service method wrong, can invalidate the lien you later file.

**Read § 38-22-109(3) directly at https://leg.colorado.gov/colorado-revised-statutes before you serve the NOI.** The statute text controls, not our summary.

## Who must be served

If you were the original contractor (directly hired by the owner), serve:
- The property owner, at the owner's address of record.

If you are a subcontractor, sub-subcontractor, or material supplier, serve:
- The property owner, at the owner's address of record, **and**
- The principal contractor (the party directly hired by the owner).

Both must be served. Serving only one is not sufficient.

## How to serve

Certified mail with return receipt requested is the standard method most Colorado contractors use. It provides admissible evidence of delivery and is accepted across Colorado counties. Alternative methods include personal service via a process server (typically $50–$100 per address) or sheriff's service — both create stronger evidence but cost more.

## Costs

Certified mail with return receipt runs roughly $8–$12 per recipient. If you are a subcontractor serving both the owner and the principal contractor, plan for $16–$24 in mailing costs for a single round of NOIs.

## The 10-day waiting period

The 10-day clock runs from the date of **receipt**, not the date mailed. That is why the green card matters: the date the recipient signs for the letter is the day the clock starts.

If the recipient refuses delivery, or does not claim the certified mail, the rules become more complicated. Refused or unclaimed mail is generally treated as delivered under Colorado service rules, but call an attorney if this happens — the lien you later file can be attacked on service grounds if the proof of delivery is shaky.

## What to keep

For every NOI you serve, keep:
- A copy of the NOI (write the certified mail Article Number on the copy).
- The certified mail receipt (Form 3800).
- The green card return receipt (Form 3811) once it comes back.
- A printout of the USPS tracking page showing date of delivery.

## The yellow callouts in your NOI document

Your NOI filing document contains yellow "CUSTOMER VERIFICATION REQUIRED" callouts in these places:

- **At the top of the document**: a reminder to verify the 10-day notice language against current § 38-22-109(3). The statutory language evolves; your template reflects the version the kit was written against. If the statute has been amended since, update the language.
- **Legal description**: if you did not have the property's legal description during intake, the callout instructs you to look it up. See Appendix B — Legal Description Lookup.
- **Owner block**: if you did not have the property owner's name and mailing address during intake, the callout directs you to your county assessor for the current owner of record. See Appendix A — County Filing Directory.

## After serving the NOI

Wait 10 days from the date of the latest-received green card. On day 11, you are eligible to file the Statement of Mechanic's Lien (Step 3). Do not wait longer than necessary — the 4-month filing deadline under § 38-22-109(5) is running from the last day of work, not from when you served the NOI.

If you have questions about this step, call a Colorado-licensed attorney.
