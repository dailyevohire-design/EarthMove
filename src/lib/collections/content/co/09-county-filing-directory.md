# Appendix A — County Filing Directory

This packet ships support for 15 Colorado counties. For each, the directory below lists the county assessor URL (for looking up owner and legal description) and the recorder URL (for filing the lien statement). Filing fees, phone numbers, and exact counter hours vary by county and change over time — call or check the county website before going in person.

**Rule of thumb:** filing fees in Colorado counties generally fall in the $13–$30 range for a 1–2 page lien statement. Do not rely on a specific number without confirming by phone — fees change.

## E-recording

Some Colorado counties accept e-recording through third-party services (e.g. Simplifile, CSC eRecording, ePN). Whether a specific county does, and whether walk-in filers are handled differently, changes; call the recorder before assuming e-recording is available.

## Filing in person

Most Colorado recorder's offices accept walk-in filings during business hours (typically Monday through Friday, 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM or 5:00 PM; some close at noon on Fridays; a few are open Saturdays for short hours). Bring:
- The notarized lien statement (original).
- A check or cash for the filing fee.
- A photocopy of the lien for your records.

The recorder stamps the original with a reception number, date, and time. Keep the stamped original. Make several photocopies before you file; the recorder's stamped copy is proof of filing.

## Filing by mail

Most counties accept filings by mail if you include:
- The notarized lien statement (original).
- A check for the filing fee, payable to the county recorder.
- A self-addressed stamped envelope so the recorded copy can be returned to you.

Use certified mail with return receipt requested for the mailing itself — you want evidence that the recorder received it in time for the 4-month deadline.

## Counties outside this list

If your property is in a Colorado county not listed above, the process is the same — call the county recorder and confirm the filing fee, accepted methods, and any local formatting requirements. Many smaller Colorado counties have unique quirks (page size, top margin, indexing requirements) — call before you drive.

If you have questions about filing in any county, call a Colorado-licensed attorney.
