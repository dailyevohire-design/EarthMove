# Glossary

Plain-English definitions of terms used in this kit and in Colorado's mechanic's lien statute. When a definition references a specific Colorado Revised Statute section, the text is available at **https://leg.colorado.gov/colorado-revised-statutes**.

**Acknowledgment** — The notary form where the signer acknowledges that they signed the document voluntarily. Distinct from a jurat.

**Claimant** — The person or company making a claim for payment. You, in this kit.

**Certified Mail with Return Receipt Requested** — USPS service that provides a mailing receipt (Form 3800) and a signed delivery card (Form 3811, "green card"). The standard method of service for demand letters and Notices of Intent in Colorado.

**Encumbrance** — Any legal claim or lien against the property that shows up in a title search. A mechanic's lien is an encumbrance.

**Excessive Lien** — A lien filed for more than is actually owed. C.R.S. § 38-22-128 makes the contractor liable for damages if they file an excessive or false lien.

**Foreclosure** — The lawsuit that converts a recorded lien into a court-ordered sale of the property. Requires an attorney.

**Jurat** — The notary form where the signer swears or affirms under oath that the contents of the document are true. Used for lien statements.

**Legal Description** — The formal lot-block-subdivision or metes-and-bounds description of a parcel. Not the street address. Required on the lien statement.

**Lien** — A legal claim against property that remains attached until paid or released. A recorded mechanic's lien is visible on any title search.

**Lis Pendens** — A recorded notice that a lawsuit is pending that affects title to the property. Filed during foreclosure.

**Material Supplier** — A company that supplied materials (lumber, concrete, gravel, etc.) to the project but did not perform labor. Material suppliers have mechanic's lien rights in Colorado under C.R.S. § 38-22-101.

**Notary Public** — A state-licensed officer who verifies identity and witnesses signatures. Available at banks, UPS Stores, AAA offices, law firms, and through mobile notary services.

**Notice of Intent (NOI)** — The 10-day notice required by C.R.S. § 38-22-109(3) before filing a lien statement. Must be served on the property owner and, for subcontractors and suppliers, on the principal contractor.

**Original Contractor** — The contractor hired directly by the property owner. Also called the "prime contractor" or "general contractor."

**Principal Contractor** — Synonym for the original contractor in Colorado's statute.

**Property Owner** — The owner of record as of the date you file the lien. Confirm through the county assessor before filing.

**Recording** — The act of filing a document with the county recorder's office, making it part of the public real estate records.

**Release of Lien** — The document you file when a lien is paid or otherwise resolved, removing the encumbrance from the property. Not included in this kit.

**Respondent** — The party you are making a claim against (the party who owes you money directly). In the demand letter and NOI, the respondent is addressed.

**Return Receipt Requested** — The USPS green-card service that produces a signed delivery acknowledgment.

**Service of Process** — The legal act of delivering a notice or summons to the recipient in a manner recognized by law. Certified mail with return receipt is a commonly accepted method for lien-adjacent notices.

**Slander of Title** — A tort claim that property owners sometimes bring against lien claimants when the lien is allegedly invalid or filed in bad faith. A real risk if you file a sloppy lien.

**Spurious Lien** — A lien filed with no legal basis. C.R.S. § 38-22-128 makes this a specific statutory tort in Colorado with double-damage exposure.

**Statement of Mechanic's Lien** — The lien document itself, filed with the county recorder.

**Subcontractor** — A contractor hired by the original contractor (not directly by the owner). Subcontractors must serve the NOI on both the owner and the original contractor.

**Sub-subcontractor** — A contractor hired by a subcontractor. In Colorado, these are also covered by § 38-22-109 for NOI requirements.

**Verified / Sworn** — Signed under oath before a notary public. A verified lien statement is required in Colorado.

If a term used in your documents is not defined here, call a Colorado-licensed attorney before acting on it.
