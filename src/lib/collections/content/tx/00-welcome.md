# Welcome to Your Contractor Payment Kit — Texas

Thank you for your purchase. Your kit contains this instruction packet and a set of filing documents tailored to your Texas fact pattern. Depending on your answers during intake, you may have received either a **full kit** (four documents) or a **demand-only kit** (two documents) — the cover page of this packet tells you which.

**This kit is not ready to file as-is.** The filing documents you are about to download include visible yellow "CUSTOMER VERIFICATION REQUIRED" callouts wherever Texas law requires you to confirm specific language against the current statute. Before you file anything, you will:

- Read this instruction packet all the way through — Texas mechanic's lien law is denser than most states.
- Determine whether the property is a **homestead** (see Step 1a). The answer changes everything.
- Determine your **contractor tier** (see Step 1b). Original contractors, subcontractors, sub-subcontractors, and material suppliers have different notice regimes in Texas.
- Look up every statute section referenced in a yellow callout at **https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov** and confirm the language in your document matches the current statute.
- Fill in any blanks (including the property's legal description, if not already entered).
- Sign the lien affidavit in front of a notary public (see the Notary Guide section).
- Send pre-lien notices and demand letters by certified mail with return receipt requested (see the Certified Mail How-To section).
- File the lien affidavit with the county clerk in the county where the property is located.
- Send a copy of the filed affidavit to the property owner within the statutory timeframe (see Step 4).

**What this service is and is not.** Earth Pro Connect LLC is not a law firm. We do not review your case. We do not give you legal advice about whether to file, how much to demand, or what happens if the owner disputes the lien. Texas lien law has many traps; the Iron Law is that improperly filed liens can expose you to liability under Tex. Prop. Code § 53.156 (fraudulent lien claim), and to slander-of-title claims at common law. If your facts are complex, or if you are past any deadline, most contractors in this situation call a Texas-licensed attorney before using a kit like this one.

**Time and cost to complete.** Most Texas contractors using this kit spend 5–12 hours of their own time across 1–4 months (the calendar time is mostly because Texas subcontractors send monthly pre-lien notices for each unpaid month). Out-of-pocket costs beyond what you paid for this kit:

- Notary: $5–$25 per signing (UPS Store, bank, AAA office).
- Certified mail with return receipt: roughly $8–$12 per mailing. Texas subcontractors and suppliers may send several.
- County clerk filing fee for the lien affidavit: varies by county; see Appendix A. Most Texas DFW counties fall in the $25–$40 range for a 1–2 page affidavit, but confirm by calling the clerk.

**What this kit does not cover.** Foreclosing on the lien (requires an attorney; Texas lien foreclosures are their own animal), removal-of-lien proceedings under Tex. Prop. Code ch. 53 subchapter J, bond-for-lien procedures under Subchapter H, residential homestead lien filings without a pre-work spouse-signed contract (see Step 1a — your kit is demand-only in that case), public property liens, any scenario requiring fund-trapping outside the basic notice, and any case past a statutory deadline.

If you have questions about what is or is not included in this kit, call a Texas-licensed attorney before proceeding.
