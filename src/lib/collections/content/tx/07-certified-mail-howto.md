# Certified Mail How-To

Your Texas pre-lien notices, demand letter, and copy-to-owner mailings all need to be sent by certified mail with return receipt requested. This section walks through the exact USPS procedure. The whole process takes about 10 minutes at a post office counter.

## What to bring to the post office

- The sealed envelope, addressed to the recipient.
- Two USPS forms: **Form 3800** (certified mail receipt — green and white) and **Form 3811** (return receipt — the "green card").
- Payment. Estimated total cost: roughly $8–$12 per mailing for a standard letter-sized envelope.

## Filling out Form 3800

- Write the recipient's name and address at the top.
- Leave the Article Number / tracking barcode alone — it is pre-printed.
- Remove the sticker and stick it to the envelope, above the recipient's address.
- Keep the bottom portion of the form with the Article Number.

## Filling out Form 3811 (green card)

- Front: recipient's name and address. Check "Certified Mail" box.
- Back: **your** name and address — this is where the signed card returns.
- Write the Article Number from Form 3800 on the green card.
- Seal the green card into the pouch on the back of the envelope, or attach with the adhesive strip.

## Tracking delivery

Every certified mail piece has a 20-digit Article Number. Track at https://tools.usps.com/go/TrackConfirmAction. The page shows acceptance, arrival at destination post office, and delivery date.

Print the tracking page for every mailing once delivery is confirmed.

## When the green card comes back

A few days to two weeks after delivery, USPS mails the green card back with:
- The recipient's signature.
- The date of delivery.

**The date of delivery is what starts the deadline clocks** in Texas lien law — especially for § 53.056 pre-lien notice deadlines. File the green card in your case folder.

## Multiple recipients (common in Texas)

Texas pre-lien notices typically go to both the property owner and the original contractor. That means two certified mailings per month per unpaid period. If you are sending § 53.056 notices for three unpaid months, you send six certified mailings. Budget the cost and the counter time accordingly.

## If the green card does not return

The USPS tracking page printout (showing "Delivered") is acceptable secondary evidence of delivery in most Texas contexts, but not universally. If your case is contested and service is questioned, call an attorney.

## If mail is refused or unclaimed

"Refused" mail is typically treated as delivered for Texas service purposes. "Unclaimed" mail is more complicated. Keep the returned envelope **unopened** with USPS notations intact. Most contractors in this situation consult a Texas-licensed attorney before relying on refused/unclaimed service for lien purposes.

## Alternative methods

- **Personal service by a process server**: $50–$100 per recipient. Creates the strongest record (affidavit of service).
- **Registered Mail**: more expensive than certified, rarely used for lien notices.
- **Constable or sheriff's service**: varies.

## What not to use

- **Certificate of Mailing** — proves you mailed it, not that anyone received it. Insufficient for § 53.056.
- **Regular first-class mail** — no proof of delivery. Can supplement but not replace certified mail.

## Record-keeping

For each certified mailing, keep:
- Copy of the document.
- Form 3800 receipt with Article Number.
- Form 3811 green card (once returned).
- Tracking page printout.

If you have questions about this step, call a Texas-licensed attorney.
