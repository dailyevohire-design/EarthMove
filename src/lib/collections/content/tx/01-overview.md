# Texas Mechanic's Lien Process Overview

Texas mechanic's lien law lives in the Texas Property Code, chapter 53. The statute text is free and public. Most contractors in this situation benefit from reading the three or four sections that apply to their fact pattern directly at **https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov** — open Property Code, then chapter 53.

Texas mechanic's lien law is substantially more technical than most states. The rules depend on:
- Whether the property is a **homestead** (see Step 1a).
- Your **contractor tier** — original contractor, subcontractor, sub-subcontractor, or material supplier (see Step 1b).
- Whether the property is **residential** or **commercial** — the pre-lien notice deadlines differ.

Before reading the chronological process below, read Steps 1a and 1b.

## The general process, chronological

**Day 0 — Last day of work.**
Multiple deadlines run from this date. The specific deadlines depend on your contractor tier.

**Each month during and after the project — Pre-lien notices (§ 53.056).**
For subcontractors, sub-subcontractors, and material suppliers, Texas requires monthly pre-lien notices. Commercial: the notice is due by the 15th day of the **3rd** month after the month of unpaid labor or materials. Residential: the notice is due by the 15th day of the **2nd** month after.

Original contractors generally do not send § 53.056 notices on non-homestead commercial property. (This is one of the most important distinctions in Texas lien law — see Step 1b.)

**Day X — Send the demand letter.**
Not strictly required by statute. Most Texas contractors still send one because it resolves a meaningful fraction of cases without further escalation.

**Before the statutory deadline — File the lien affidavit (§ 53.052, § 53.054).**
- **Commercial non-residential, original contractor**: file no later than the 15th day of the 4th month after the last day of work.
- **Commercial non-residential, sub or supplier**: file no later than the 15th day of the 4th month after the month of the last day of work.
- **Residential, all tiers**: file no later than the 15th day of the 3rd month after the last day of work.

(These are the general rules. Edge cases — retainage, specially fabricated materials, certain contracted indebtedness provisions — have their own timing rules. Read § 53.052 directly.)

**Within 10 days of filing (§ 53.055).**
Texas requires the lien claimant to send a copy of the filed lien affidavit to the property owner. For subs and suppliers, send a copy to the original contractor too. Missing this deadline can affect the lien's validity.

**One year from date of last labor on the improvement — foreclosure deadline (§ 53.158).**
For residential projects the deadline is shorter (typically one year or until one year from the notice of filing, whichever is earlier). Foreclosure is a lawsuit in district court and requires an attorney.

## Visual timeline (commercial, sub/supplier, monthly)

```
Month M (unpaid) : Send pre-lien notice by 15th of M+3 (commercial)
                    or 15th of M+2 (residential)
Last day of work : Clock starts on 4-month filing deadline
Day 0 + 4 months : File lien affidavit by 15th of 4th month after
Within 10 days  : Send copy to owner (+ OC if applicable)
Within 1 year   : File foreclosure lawsuit or lien expires
```

## What differs from Colorado

If you've used a Colorado kit before (or you are familiar with Colorado): Texas is not the same.

- Texas uses a **monthly notice regime** for subs and suppliers, not a single "10-day notice of intent."
- Texas has a **fund-trapping** mechanism (§§ 53.081–.084) that Colorado does not.
- Texas **homestead rules** are substantially more protective than Colorado's.
- Texas has a **retainage** regime (§ 53.101) with its own rules, not applicable to every project but sometimes critical.
- The statute of limitations to foreclose is **longer in Texas** (one year generally, vs. six months in Colorado), but the **pre-lien notice deadlines are shorter**.

If you are not sure how the above applies to your project, call a Texas-licensed attorney before using the filing documents in this kit.
