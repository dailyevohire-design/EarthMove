# Glossary

Plain-English definitions of terms used in this kit and in Texas lien law.

**Affidavit of Lien** — The Texas document filed with the county clerk to perfect a mechanic's lien. Governed by Tex. Prop. Code § 53.054.

**Certified Mail with Return Receipt Requested** — USPS service providing a mailing receipt and a signed delivery card ("green card"). The standard method for Texas pre-lien notices, demand letters, and copy-to-owner mailings.

**County Appraisal District (CAD)** — The Texas agency that lists property owners, assessed values, and legal descriptions for every parcel in a county. Each county has its own CAD with a public website.

**County Clerk** — The Texas county office where real property documents are filed, including lien affidavits. Distinct from the appraisal district.

**Fund-Trapping** — A Texas statutory mechanism (Tex. Prop. Code §§ 53.081–.084) that, when invoked in a pre-lien notice, effectively requires the property owner to withhold payments from the general contractor equal to the claimed amount. Aggressive; consult counsel before using.

**Homestead** — Property occupied by the owner as their primary residence, protected from most forced sales under Tex. Const. art. XVI § 50 and Tex. Prop. Code ch. 41. Homestead liens require special formalities; see Step 1a.

**Jurat** — The notary form where the signer swears or affirms under oath that the contents are true. Required for a Texas lien affidavit.

**Legal Description** — The lot/block/subdivision or metes-and-bounds description of a parcel. Required or strongly recommended on the lien affidavit.

**Lien** — A legal claim against property that remains until paid or released. A recorded lien is visible on any title search.

**Material Supplier** — A Texas contractor tier; supplies materials without performing labor. See Step 1b.

**Notary Public** — A Texas-licensed officer who verifies identity and witnesses signatures. Available at banks, UPS Stores, AAA offices, credit unions, and through mobile and online notary services.

**Original Contractor** — The contractor directly hired by the property owner. Also called the "general contractor" or "prime contractor." See Step 1b.

**Pre-Lien Notice** — The monthly notice required by Tex. Prop. Code § 53.056 for subcontractors, sub-subcontractors, and material suppliers. Due by the 15th day of the 3rd month after unpaid commercial work, or 2nd month after unpaid residential work.

**Property Owner** — The record owner of the property as shown in county clerk records. Confirm through the CAD before filing.

**Recording** — Filing a document with the county clerk, making it part of the public real property records.

**Release of Lien** — The document filed when a lien is paid or resolved, removing it from title. Tex. Prop. Code § 53.152 sets timing requirements. Not included in this kit.

**Respondent** — The party you are making a claim against (the party who owes you money directly).

**Retainage** — Amounts withheld from contract payments until completion, under § 53.101. Has its own separate notice and filing rules.

**Service of Process** — The legal act of delivering a notice or document to the recipient in a manner recognized by law. Certified mail with return receipt is commonly accepted for § 53.056 notices and copy-to-owner mailings.

**Slander of Title** — A Texas common-law tort claim for damages caused by the recording of a false or unjustified lien. Real risk for invalid filings.

**Spousal Signatures** — Required on Texas homestead improvement contracts. Both spouses must sign the pre-work contract, regardless of who holds title.

**Subchapter H** — The Texas Property Code provisions allowing the property owner to bond around a lien, effectively removing it from title while the claim survives against the bond.

**Subchapter J** — The Texas Property Code provisions allowing expedited court removal of allegedly invalid liens. Very short response deadlines; get an attorney immediately if served.

**Subcontractor** — A Texas contractor tier; hired by the original contractor rather than the owner. See Step 1b.

**Sub-subcontractor** — A contractor hired by a subcontractor. See Step 1b.

**Tex. Prop. Code § 53.156** — Texas's fraudulent-lien statute. Penalties (damages, attorney fees) against a claimant who files an affidavit they know to be fraudulent. The Iron Law.

**Verified / Sworn** — Signed under oath before a notary public. Required for a Texas lien affidavit.

If a term in your documents is not defined here, call a Texas-licensed attorney before acting on it.
